# GymRats Vision & Culture Guide

This document captures the core vision, cultural positioning, and personality of GymRats.

---

## The Vision

**"GymRats is a workout tracking app that feels alive."**

Unlike competitors (Strong, Hevy, Liftoff) that feel like sterile spreadsheets, GymRats combines functional efficiency with emotional personality. The app doesn't just track your workouts — it celebrates your progress, grows with you, and connects you to a community that gets lifting culture.

---

## The Core Insight

> "There's no lifting app that 'gets' lifting culture. It's all so safe and sterile and out of touch."

GymRats exists because every other workout app is functional but soulless. They're tools. GymRats is a companion.

---

## Cultural Positioning

### What We Embrace

**Bodybuilding Culture**
- The discipline, the aesthetics, the pump
- Not just powerlifting, not just strength — the full culture
- The gym as a place of transformation

**Online Fitness Culture**
- Memes, influencer content, terminally online humor
- The way lifting culture lives on social media
- Inside jokes that lifters understand

**Raw Authenticity**
- Don't sterilize it, don't corporate-speak it
- Real talk about the grind
- Acknowledge the struggle and the triumph

### What We're NOT

- Sterile health app designed by people who don't lift
- "Wellness" corporate speak
- Judgmental or gatekeeping
- Cringe (we're self-aware)

---

## Tone of Voice

### In the App

**Buddy Messages (varies by personality)**
- Coach: Supportive, knowledgeable, encouraging
- Hype Beast: LOUD, over-the-top, energy
- Savage: Brutally honest, dark humor, roasts with love
- Goth Gym Rat: Deadpan, ironic, very online

**System Messages**
- Conversational, not corporate
- Celebratory on achievements
- Understanding on missed days (no guilt trips)

### Examples

Good:
- "That's a new PR. You earned it."
- "Back at it after 3 days? Let's get to work."
- "Your bench is climbing. Keep the momentum."

Bad:
- "Congratulations on your achievement!"
- "You haven't logged a workout in 3 days. Don't lose your streak!"
- "Great job! You're doing amazing!"

## Inclusivity Philosophy

### The Balance

**Welcoming onboarding** → **Authentic core experience**

- New users should feel welcomed and supported
- Once they're in, they experience the real culture
- Edginess is opt-in (through buddy selection)
- Never gatekeeping, always inviting

### Representation

- Gender-neutral avatar options standard
- All body types represented in art
- Multiple skin tones
- No assumptions about user goals

### Not "Gym Bro" Exclusive

- GymRats is for everyone who lifts, regardless of:
  - Experience level (beginner to advanced)
  - Gender
  - Body type
  - Training style (strength, hypertrophy, general fitness)
- The culture we celebrate is the shared love of lifting, not toxic gatekeeping

## The Differentiators

### 1. GymRats Scoring

Your rank means something. Built on verified, static standards — not user submissions that inflate over time. When you hit Gold rank, you're actually at a Gold level compared to real strength standards.

### 2. AI Gym Buddy

Not a chatbot. A personality. Like a sports announcer who reacts to your lifts in real-time. From encouraging Coach to brutal Savage, you pick your vibe.

### 3. Avatar & Hangout

Finch-style growing companion + shared space with friends. Your avatar grows as you grow. See your friends in your room, know when they're working out. Emotional investment in your progress.

### 4. Workout Replay

THE share moment. Cinematic post-workout summary with your stats, PRs, buddy commentary. Made to be shared, made to look good.

### 5. Culture-First Design

Every detail — copy, animations, buddies, reactions — designed by people who get lifting culture. Inside jokes land. The vibe is right.

## What Success Looks Like

**A new user's journey:**

1. Downloads because it looks different (not another sterile tracker)
2. Picks a buddy that matches their personality
3. Logs their first workout, gets excited by PR detection
4. Shares a Workout Replay, friends comment
5. Sees their avatar grow, feels invested
6. Keeps coming back because the app celebrates them
7. Recommends to lifting friends because "this app gets it"

**The feeling we create:**

- "Finally, an app that doesn't feel corporate"
- "My buddy actually hypes me up"
- "I want to hit a PR just to see what the app does"
- "My friend saw my Replay and downloaded it"

## Guiding Questions

When making decisions, ask:

1. **Does this feel authentic to lifting culture?**
2. **Would a lifter be excited by this?**
3. **Is this sterile or does it have personality?**
4. **Does this celebrate the user?**
5. **Would someone share this?**
